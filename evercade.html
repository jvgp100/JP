My cart comments

Console

22. Bitmap Brothers collection

So, the Bitmap Brothers. Yes, them. Surely one of the original 'rockstar' development houses, their glory days were in the early 90s on the Amiga. But, this being a console collection, this is an assortment of Master System / Mega Drive / SNES / PS1 stuff.

Highlight(s)? Well, it has to be the Chaos Engine. This is a top-down arcade-style shooter. It's hard as balls, but has amazing music and an ahead-of-its-time steampunk art-style that really suits the whole vibe, man. Even better with two players.

What about the rest? Speedball 2: HD is a similarly iconic game that people "raved about" back in the day. Basically futuristic ice hockey, sort of. Needs two players even more than the Chaos Engine, though. Playing against AI gets old, fast. We also have Speedball (the original) on Master System, which is like Speedball 2 but clunkier and jankier. Then there's Speedball 2100 on PS1, which is like Speedball 2 but less charming. Then there's Xenon 2, which is an okay-ish SHMUP. But there are better examples elsewhere on Evercade (especially the Arcade collections), so you won't want to spend much time with it.

Will I pick it up again? Yes, I reckon so - but maybe only if I have friends round. The best games on here (Speedball 2 and Chaos Engine) are both immeasurably improved by the presence of some "good mates" and perhaps "a cold one".

Arcade

1. Technos Arcade collection

Technos? Who they? I'll tell you. The people who brought the world DOUBLE-DRAGON, that's who. And also any other game you remember featuring those weird blocky dudes who apparently are called Kunio-Kun. The most notable, as far as I am concerned, being Nintendo World Cup, which I ploughed hours into back on the Gameboy.

Highlight(s)? Minky Monkey. Apparently a Donkey Kong Jr. clone, but I haven't played that. So as far as I am concerned, this is a breathtakingly original game from 1982 which is so obscure it doesn't have a Wikipedia page. Unbelievable! You have to complete tasks given to you by a Monkey. These range from "bring up the strawberry" to "bring down the banana". Kind of hard to explain. Anyway, I love these ancient arcade games. They are just raw fun, from back in the day when that is all that developers could give you, because no-one had invented graphics yet.
